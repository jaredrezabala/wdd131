Ecuador is a country located in northwestern South America, bordered by Colombia to the north and Peru to the east and south, with the Pacific Ocean to the west. It gained independence from Spain in 1822 after the Battle of Pichincha. The country is known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse geography—including the Andes Mountains, the Amazon rainforest, and the Galápagos Islands—and for being named after the equator, which runs through its territory.
